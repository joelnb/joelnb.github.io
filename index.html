Joel Noyce Barnham About me

This is just a simple website to provide some information about who I am and how to contact me. I am a Computer Science graduate with first-class honours from the University of Birmingham with interests focussing on security, privacy and the intersection of technology and intellectual property laws.

At university I have completed modules covering a large number of technologies including Haskell, Java, C/C++, SQL and Matlab, meaning I am familiar with a number of different programming styles and design patterns. My final year project was based around the idea of keyboard emanations which has been previously explored by Asonov & Agrawal in their paper 'Keyboard Acoustic Emanations' and a number of other researchers in later papers. This involved working with different signal processing techniques attempting to separate useful data from the noise in accelerometer data.

Before university I studied at Kingdown School where I acheived A-levels in Maths, Physics and German.
